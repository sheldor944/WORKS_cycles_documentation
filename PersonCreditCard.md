# PersonCreditCard

---

## 📋 Short Description

### Style 1: Conceptual Summary
This table is a junction/bridge table linking people to their credit cards — one row per person-card combination (19,118 records). It captures which person (BusinessEntityID → Person) owns which credit card (CreditCardID → CreditCard). A person can have multiple cards and theoretically a card could be linked to multiple people. This is the ownership bridge between the Person identity model and the CreditCard payment model — while SalesOrderHeader.CreditCardID tracks which card was *used* for a specific order, this table tracks which cards a person *has on file*.

### Style 2: Query Possibilities & Business Story
This is the card ownership table — it connects people to the credit cards they have registered in the system. It's the "wallet" view of each person's payment methods. Use this table to answer questions like:

- "How many credit cards does each person have on file?"
- "Which people have multiple cards registered?"
- "How many people have at least one card vs. no card?"
- "What card types does a specific customer have?"
- "Are there credit cards not linked to any person?" (orphaned cards)
- "Are there people with cards on file who have never placed an order?"
- "Which card type is most commonly owned by employees vs. customers?" (with Person, Employee)
- "What's the average number of cards per person by person type?"
- "How many people own expired cards?" (with CreditCard.ExpMonth/ExpYear)
- "For customers who placed orders, did they use a card that's linked to them here — or a different card?" (with SalesOrderHeader)
- "What's the revenue generated by each person's cards?" (with SalesOrderHeader)
- "Which people have cards but have opted out of email promotions?" (with Person.EmailPromotion)

This is a pure bridge table with no business data beyond the link itself. Its value is in connecting the Person and CreditCard entities and enabling wallet-level analysis.

### Style 3: Grouped & Complete (Column Reference)
This table stores one row per person-credit card assignment (19,118 rows), containing 3 columns organized as:

- **Composite PK:** BusinessEntityID (FK → Person), CreditCardID (FK → CreditCard)
- **Audit:** ModifiedDate

## 📖 Extensive Description

### Purpose & Business Context

PersonCreditCard is the many-to-many junction table connecting people to their credit cards. With 19,118 records linking to 19,972 people and 19,118 credit cards, the data suggests a near 1:1 relationship in practice — most people who have a card have exactly one, and most cards belong to exactly one person. However, the schema supports multiple cards per person and (theoretically) shared cards.

This table represents card **ownership/registration**, not card **usage**. Card usage is tracked at the order level via SalesOrderHeader.CreditCardID. This distinction is important:
- PersonCreditCard: "This person **has** this card on file"
- SalesOrderHeader: "This order **was paid with** this card"

A person could have a card registered here but never use it for an order, or could use a card for orders that isn't registered here (though that would be unusual).

### Key Business Logic

- **Composite PK (BusinessEntityID, CreditCardID)** — one person can have multiple cards, one card could theoretically link to multiple people
- **19,118 rows ≈ 19,118 CreditCard rows** — suggests 1:1 between cards and this junction (each card is linked exactly once)
- **19,118 < 19,972 Person rows** — ~854 people have NO card on file
- **Card ownership vs. usage** — this table = ownership; SalesOrderHeader.CreditCardID = transactional usage
- **ModifiedDate** = when the person-card link was created or last modified
- **No business data** — pure bridge; all card details (type, number, expiration) are in CreditCard
- **Not all people with cards are customers** — employees and contacts may also have cards registered

### Relationships

| Direction | Table | Join Key | Notes |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------|
| → Parent | Person → BusinessEntity | BusinessEntityID | Card owner |
| → Parent | CreditCard | CreditCardID | The card itself (type, number, expiration) |
| ← Via Person | Employee | BusinessEntityID | Employees who own cards |
| ← Via Person | Customer (via PersonID) | BusinessEntityID | Customers who own cards |
| ← Via Person | EmailAddress | BusinessEntityID | Card owner's email |
| ← Via Person | BusinessEntityAddress → Address | BusinessEntityID | Card owner's address |
| ← Via CreditCard | SalesOrderHeader | CreditCardID | Orders paid with this card |
| ← Via SalesOrderHeader | SalesOrderDetail → Product | SalesOrderID | What was bought with this card |
| ← Via SalesOrderHeader | SalesTerritory | TerritoryID | Where orders with this card originated |

### Join-Heavy Queries (5)

**Q1: "For each person with a credit card, show their full name, person type, email, card type, card expiration, and whether the card is expired."**

*5 joins: PersonCreditCard → Person → EmailAddress → CreditCard*

```sql
SELECT
    per.FirstName || ' ' || per.LastName AS FullName,
    per.PersonType,
    CASE per.PersonType
        WHEN 'EM' THEN 'Employee'
        WHEN 'SP' THEN 'Sales Person'
        WHEN 'IN' THEN 'Individual Customer'
        WHEN 'SC' THEN 'Store Contact'
        WHEN 'VC' THEN 'Vendor Contact'
        WHEN 'GC' THEN 'General Contact'
    END AS PersonTypeLabel,
    ea.EmailAddress,
    cc.CardType,
    cc.ExpMonth || '/' || cc.ExpYear AS Expiration,
    CASE
        WHEN cc.ExpYear < CAST(strftime('%Y', 'now') AS INTEGER)
            OR (cc.ExpYear = CAST(strftime('%Y', 'now') AS INTEGER)
                AND cc.ExpMonth < CAST(strftime('%m', 'now') AS INTEGER))
        THEN 'EXPIRED'
        ELSE 'ACTIVE'
    END AS CardStatus
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN EmailAddress ea ON per.BusinessEntityID = ea.BusinessEntityID
ORDER BY per.LastName, per.FirstName;
```

**Q2: "For each customer with a card on file, show their name, email, card type, home address (city/state/country), total orders placed with that card, and total revenue."**

*10 joins: PersonCreditCard → Person → EmailAddress → CreditCard → Customer → SalesOrderHeader → BusinessEntityAddress → Address → StateProvince → CountryRegion*

```sql
SELECT
    per.FirstName || ' ' || per.LastName AS CustomerName,
    ea.EmailAddress,
    cc.CardType,
    a.City,
    sp.Name AS State,
    cr.Name AS Country,
    COUNT(DISTINCT soh.SalesOrderID) AS OrdersWithThisCard,
    ROUND(SUM(soh.TotalDue), 2) AS RevenueFromThisCard
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN EmailAddress ea ON per.BusinessEntityID = ea.BusinessEntityID
JOIN Customer c ON per.BusinessEntityID = c.PersonID
LEFT JOIN SalesOrderHeader soh ON c.CustomerID = soh.CustomerID
    AND soh.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN BusinessEntityAddress bea ON per.BusinessEntityID = bea.BusinessEntityID
LEFT JOIN Address a ON bea.AddressID = a.AddressID
LEFT JOIN StateProvince sp ON a.StateProvinceID = sp.StateProvinceID
LEFT JOIN CountryRegion cr ON sp.CountryRegionCode = cr.CountryRegionCode
GROUP BY per.FirstName, per.LastName, ea.EmailAddress, cc.CardType,
         a.City, sp.Name, cr.Name
ORDER BY RevenueFromThisCard DESC;
```

**Q3: "For each person with a card, show their name, card type, the products they've purchased with that card (by category), total quantity, and total spend."**

*9 joins: PersonCreditCard → Person → CreditCard → Customer → SalesOrderHeader → SalesOrderDetail → Product → ProductSubcategory → ProductCategory*

```sql
SELECT
    per.FirstName || ' ' || per.LastName AS PersonName,
    cc.CardType,
    pcat.Name AS ProductCategory,
    SUM(sod.OrderQty) AS TotalQtyPurchased,
    ROUND(SUM(sod.LineTotal), 2) AS TotalSpend,
    COUNT(DISTINCT soh.SalesOrderID) AS OrderCount
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
JOIN Customer c ON per.BusinessEntityID = c.PersonID
JOIN SalesOrderHeader soh ON c.CustomerID = soh.CustomerID
    AND soh.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
JOIN SalesOrderDetail sod ON soh.SalesOrderID = sod.SalesOrderID
JOIN Product p ON sod.ProductID = p.ProductID
LEFT JOIN ProductSubcategory psub ON p.ProductSubcategoryID = psub.ProductSubcategoryID
LEFT JOIN ProductCategory pcat ON psub.ProductCategoryID = pcat.ProductCategoryID
GROUP BY per.FirstName, per.LastName, cc.CardType, pcat.Name
ORDER BY TotalSpend DESC;
```

**Q4: "For people with cards, show their name, card type, the salesperson who handled their orders, the salesperson's territory, and the ship method used — a full payment-to-delivery view."**

*10 joins: PersonCreditCard → Person → CreditCard → Customer → SalesOrderHeader → SalesPerson → Employee → Person(rep) → SalesTerritory → ShipMethod*

```sql
SELECT
    cust.FirstName || ' ' || cust.LastName AS CustomerName,
    cc.CardType,
    rep.FirstName || ' ' || rep.LastName AS SalesPersonName,
    st.Name AS Territory,
    sm.Name AS ShipMethod,
    COUNT(DISTINCT soh.SalesOrderID) AS Orders,
    ROUND(SUM(soh.TotalDue), 2) AS TotalRevenue
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person cust ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = cust.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
JOIN Customer c ON cust.BusinessEntityID = c.PersonID
JOIN SalesOrderHeader soh ON c.CustomerID = soh.CustomerID
    AND soh.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN SalesPerson salp ON soh.SalesPersonID = salp.BusinessEntityID
LEFT JOIN Employee e ON salp.BusinessEntityID = e.BusinessEntityID
LEFT JOIN Person rep ON e.BusinessEntityID = rep.BusinessEntityID
JOIN SalesTerritory st ON soh.TerritoryID = st.TerritoryID
JOIN ShipMethod sm ON soh.ShipMethodID = sm.ShipMethodID
GROUP BY cust.FirstName, cust.LastName, cc.CardType, rep.FirstName,
         rep.LastName, st.Name, sm.Name
ORDER BY TotalRevenue DESC;
```

**Q5: "For people who are both employees and have cards on file, show their name, job title, department, card type, password last changed date, and whether they've used this card as a customer."**

*9 joins: PersonCreditCard → Person → Employee → EmployeeDepartmentHistory → Department → CreditCard → Password → Customer → SalesOrderHeader*

```sql
SELECT
    per.FirstName || ' ' || per.LastName AS EmployeeName,
    e.JobTitle,
    d.Name AS Department,
    cc.CardType,
    cc.ExpMonth || '/' || cc.ExpYear AS Expiration,
    pwd.ModifiedDate AS PasswordLastChanged,
    CASE
        WHEN soh.SalesOrderID IS NOT NULL THEN 'Yes — used as customer'
        ELSE 'No — card unused for orders'
    END AS UsedForOrders,
    COUNT(DISTINCT soh.SalesOrderID) AS OrderCount
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN Employee e ON per.BusinessEntityID = e.BusinessEntityID
JOIN EmployeeDepartmentHistory edh ON e.BusinessEntityID = edh.BusinessEntityID
    AND edh.EndDate IS NULL
JOIN Department d ON edh.DepartmentID = d.DepartmentID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN Password pwd ON per.BusinessEntityID = pwd.BusinessEntityID
LEFT JOIN Customer c ON per.BusinessEntityID = c.PersonID
LEFT JOIN SalesOrderHeader soh ON c.CustomerID = soh.CustomerID
    AND soh.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
GROUP BY per.FirstName, per.LastName, e.JobTitle, d.Name, cc.CardType,
         cc.ExpMonth, cc.ExpYear, pwd.ModifiedDate
ORDER BY per.LastName;
```

**Q6: "How many cards does each person have on file, and what's the distribution?"**

*Use case: Customer profile — wallet depth analysis*

```sql
WITH CardsPerPerson AS (
    SELECT
        BusinessEntityID,
        COUNT(*) AS CardCount
    FROM PersonCreditCard
    GROUP BY BusinessEntityID
)
SELECT
    CardCount,
    COUNT(*) AS PeopleWithThisMany,
    ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM CardsPerPerson), 2) AS PctOfCardholders
FROM CardsPerPerson
GROUP BY CardCount
ORDER BY CardCount;
```

**Q7: "Which people have cards on file but have NEVER placed an order?"**

*Use case: Conversion opportunity — registered but inactive*

```sql
SELECT
    per.BusinessEntityID,
    per.FirstName || ' ' || per.LastName AS FullName,
    per.PersonType,
    cc.CardType,
    cc.ExpMonth || '/' || cc.ExpYear AS Expiration
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
LEFT JOIN Customer c ON per.BusinessEntityID = c.PersonID
```

**Q8: "Are there credit cards that exist in CreditCard but are NOT linked to any person?"**

*Use case: Data integrity — orphaned card detection*

```sql
SELECT
    cc.CreditCardID,
    cc.CardType,
    '****' || SUBSTR(cc.CardNumber, -4) AS MaskedNumber,
    cc.ExpMonth || '/' || cc.ExpYear AS Expiration
FROM CreditCard cc
LEFT JOIN PersonCreditCard pcc ON cc.CreditCardID = pcc.CreditCardID
WHERE pcc.CreditCardID IS NULL
ORDER BY cc.CardType;
```

**Q9: "What's the card type ownership breakdown by person type — which types of people hold which types of cards?"**

*Use case: Customer demographics — payment method segmentation*

```sql
SELECT
    per.PersonType,
    CASE per.PersonType
        WHEN 'EM' THEN 'Employee'
        WHEN 'SP' THEN 'Sales Person'
        WHEN 'IN' THEN 'Individual Customer'
        WHEN 'SC' THEN 'Store Contact'
        WHEN 'VC' THEN 'Vendor Contact'
        WHEN 'GC' THEN 'General Contact'
    END AS PersonTypeLabel,
    cc.CardType,
    COUNT(*) AS CardCount,
    ROUND(COUNT(*) * 100.0 / SUM(COUNT(*)) OVER (PARTITION BY per.PersonType), 2) AS PctWithinType
FROM PersonCreditCard pcc
JOIN Person per ON pcc.BusinessEntityID = per.BusinessEntityID
JOIN CreditCard cc ON pcc.CreditCardID = cc.CreditCardID
GROUP BY per.PersonType, cc.CardType
ORDER BY per.PersonType, CardCount DESC;
```

**Q10: "How many people have NO credit card at all — and how does that break down by person type?"**

*Use case: Payment coverage gap analysis*

```sql
SELECT
    per.PersonType,
    CASE per.PersonType
        WHEN 'EM' THEN 'Employee'
        WHEN 'SP' THEN 'Sales Person'
        WHEN 'IN' THEN 'Individual Customer'
        WHEN 'SC' THEN 'Store Contact'
        WHEN 'VC' THEN 'Vendor Contact'
        WHEN 'GC' THEN 'General Contact'
    END AS PersonTypeLabel,
    COUNT(*) AS PeopleWithoutCard
FROM Person per
LEFT JOIN PersonCreditCard pcc ON per.BusinessEntityID = pcc.BusinessEntityID
WHERE pcc.BusinessEntityID IS NULL
GROUP BY per.PersonType
ORDER BY PeopleWithoutCard DESC;
```
